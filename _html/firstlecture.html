First Lecture

The First Lecture was an introduction to UX and an explenation of what it is

First Lecture

Today the first lectures and seminars began in The Hague University of Applied sciencesregarding the Communication and Multimedia Design – User Experience (CMD-UX) course.

The first Seminar was an Introduction to the principles of “UX- Design”. From the start it was clear that, the name “User Experience Design” was a broad term with many definitions to it.

We stuck to the definition by Hartson & Pyla:

“User Experience is the totality of the effect or effects felt by a user as a result of interaction with, and the usage context of, a system, device, or product, including the influence of usability, usefulness, and emotional impact during interaction”

Hartson & Pyla,  2012

We also discussed the path that lead to the existence of this field of study.

Figure of The History of User Experience

1940s to 1950s
Early computers were incredibly complex to operate so the only users were highly trained engineers. It was all about evaluating if the system was reliable eugh and how long it would function.

1950s to 1960s
Computers began to get smaller and became very slightly less complicated to use.
There were also ne Inventions that enhanced usability, such as the following new input methods: magnetic tape, punch cards, light guns, and, eventually, keyboards.

Now the engineers were not the only users anymore.With the invention of programming Languages many people learned how to program and eventually computers were used by both “programmers” and “computer scientists”.

1960s to 1980s
Users were no longer trained experts; they were non- specialists

With these users, it was necessary to determine if a computer would actually be able to save time. Evaluators began to focus on user performance, which meant they would focus on task  completion and the time it took, how many errors occurred, and how difficult it would be to learn how to effectively use a computer.

1980s to 2000s
The GUI interface, pioneered by Xerox and later on used in both Microsoft and Apple products, was a huge step in Usability and visualizing data on a Computer.

Now anyone was able to use Computer systems  with minimal training and support.

2000s to present (2016)
“Recently” Computers are used for all kinds of things and ease our way of live in many aspects, some maybe:
Personal computing, social computing, mobile computing, cloud computing, and ERP Systems. These inventions and enhancements have changed how we live, communicate, work and also where and on what Hardware.

Thus, the emotional side of using computers is paramount. Although Task-based performance are still important we are realizing that using a computer is both cognitive and emotional.

It is the Experience that really matters!
