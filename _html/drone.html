Drone UX

This post will analyse the UX of a Drone Controller and highlight the important facts.

Drone UX

Drone have developed quite a lot in the recent years. Nowadays there are all kinds of drones being used for various purposes, such as e.g communication, military, research (Ethology, Geography), commercial, entertainment, video production purposes and many more.

Drones are used in all kinds of locations although they are restricted in some no-fly zones around the globe. Not all drones have a digital interface, however “camera drones” mostly do, which makes them interesting to study from the user experience point of view.

The display is important not only for knowing what you are filming, but also orientation, which implies, that the software design must be clear to the user. Since the drone is changing the point of view (POV) constantly, it is easier to concentrate on the display, rather than looking at the drone from your own POV.

Drones are interesting to explore from the UX perspective, because there is lots of potential and room for improvements. It is also possible that other products such as the “Oculus Rift” could be combined with a drone to enhance orientation when operating the drone.

The scope of users is very wide, since researchers, the military (soldiers) and video producers could all benefit from drones. But drones are not only used in a commercial way, many users just use it for fun and for capturing moments in form of a video.
