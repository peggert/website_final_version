WHAT IS THE CHARACTER OF TOURISM IN THE NETHERLANDS?

This research summary consists out of three following main sources; “The Incidence of Smartphone Usage among Tourists”, “2014 Inbound Tourism Survey” and “A Typology of Technology‐Enhanced Tourism Experiences”. With help of theses sources this summary will elaborate on the questions: “What is the character of tourism in The Netherlands” and how digital technology influences tourists holiday experiences.

1.1 WHAT ACTIVITIES DO TOURISTS IN THE NETHERLANDS UNDERTAKE?

According to the survey on inbound tourism the tourism in the netherlands had a great increase in tourists through “the re-opening of major museums and international events” . The survey also states, that tourists that are in the netherlands for leisure mostly “walk through towns and cities” (54%), “visit a bar or cafe” (45%), “funshopping” (41%) and/or visit restaurants and museums (Restaurant=40% & Museum=35%) . City walks are extremely popular as well, especially among Brazilians, Russians, Canadian and American tourists.Business travelers, like to go shopping and visiting pubs and restaurants. Furthermore business travelers like to take walks around the cities even more. The museums in The Hague are the most popular according to the survey.

1.2 WHAT DO TOURISTS LIKE AND DISLIKE ABOUT THE NETHERLANDS?

Eating in the netherlands was a very popular activity in the netherlands . Especially International tourists  were attracted by “The Van Gogh Museum, the Rijksmuseum and the Anne Frank House” according to “google reviews” visitors liked the Museums and houses”Most of the reviews were positive and people enjoyed their stay. The general appreciation of international visitors on their stay in holland  was very high according to the survey . However when participants of the survey were asked what improvements could be made to improve the quality of their stay, they answered that the information and signposting provided for the tourists could be clearer and preferably in their own language. Tourists also mentioned in the survey, that they were not pleased by the price level in Holland and expect: “cheaper accommodations, restaurants, taxis, public transport and wish a discounts for tourists. Many visitors also thought that there was space for improvements in terms of the user friendliness in  public transport and the quality of accommodations.

1.3 WHO ARE THE TOURISTS IN NETHERLANDS?

Most of the tourists originate from neighbouring countries and europe. According to the survey (2014), most tourists are coming from the neighbouring country Germany. The second most are coming from Great Britain and the third most from Belgium. From outside of Europe most visitors in the netherlands come from North America, United States. In 2014 69% of all visitors came because of leisure and 26% came because of business. Most business visitors were in the Netherlands for individual business and came mostly from Germany (86%)

1.4 HOW LONG AND WHERE DO TOURISTS STAY?

More than half of the visitors in Holland, stay one to three nights, and “one third stays over seven nights” and “over ten percent stay eight days or more” . Most of the international tourists accommodated in hotels, campsites and guest houses .  Most visitors stayed in one of the following cities: Amsterdam, Rotterdam, The Hague and Utrecht.

HOW DOES DIGITAL TECHNOLOGY INFLUENCE TOURISTS’ HOLIDAY EXPERIENCES?
2.1 HOW DOES DIGITAL TECHNOLOGY (APPS AND WEBSITES) ENHANCE TOURISTS’ HOLIDAY EXPERIENCES?

In general the most often used applications were the smartphone camera and social media apps like Facebook and Instagram. Social media enhances the travelers experience because he is able to share his experiences while travelling with the people he knows. Furthermore people looked up websites with accommodation and destination reviews. According to the journal about tourism research, maps, navigation and transportation systems are especially helpful to tourist in order to guide them throughout their holidays. During travels “technology can become part of the experience through active involvement, social media engagement pre/during/post travel.”

2.2 HOW DO TOURISTS MAKE USE OF TECHNOLOGY BEFORE AND DURING THEIR TRAVELS?

Before the rips start tourists as mentioned earlier look up websites with reviews but also look up brochures and travel guides. According to the survey conducted in Holland, many still use travel agents and operators. Furthermore the survey also supports the view that more and more visitors consult review sites these days. During the travels however the most used app is the camera.
